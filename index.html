Bosnian and Herzegovinian Genome Project (BHGP)

Advancing genomic research through inclusive representation of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

About BHGP

Following the completion of the Human Genome Project in 2003, countries around the world have advanced this initiative by contributing valuable genetic, phenotypic, and clinical data for biomedical research projects through cohort studies.

Cohorts are instrumental in examining diverse populations, key demographic subgroups, rare genotypes and exposures, as well as gene-environment interactions. However, each cohort is limited by its size, ancestral backgrounds, and geographical regions.

A major challenge in human genetics has been the historical under-representation of large segments of the population in biomedical research. As Bosnia and Herzegovina endeavors to engage in genetic research and establish its presence in the global genetic landscape, BHGP aims to contribute up to 1,000 genomes on a volunteer basis.

The objective is to deepen our understanding of the genetic composition of B&H citizens, enhance opportunities for biomedical research, and assess the prevalence of specific genetic variants associated with various diseases.

Contribution

BHGP is based on voluntary participation. Individuals interested in contributing to the project will help establish the first large-scale genomic dataset from Bosnia and Herzegovina, supporting future biomedical and population genetics research.
